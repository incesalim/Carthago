# Türkiye Economic Outlook — 1Q26 (March 2026)

Garanti BBVA Research, 44 pp. Written **during the Iran conflict** (US–Israel
attacks on Iran, March 2026) — the whole edition is framed around how long the
war lasts. Baseline assumes a **short-lived conflict**, with explicit upward
bias on inflation/rates and downward bias on growth if it persists.

Values below are as printed in the report (data mostly through ~25–27 March
2026). Items marked **[not on dashboard]** have no data source in our stack;
the rest are reproduced (see README for the tab map).

## Main messages (p. 2)

- CBRT's **implicit FX reserve sales reached >$50bn in March** (through the
  25th): ~$35bn foreign sell-off + $15–20bn domestic corporates, supported by
  gold sales and swapping gold.
- Limited monetary easing and only mild fiscal support ahead; longer/deeper
  conflict ⇒ harsher growth trade-off via external financing needs.
- 2025 growth 3.6%; 1Q26 nowcast ~3%; 2026 forecast 4.0% with downside risks.
- Disinflation decelerated **before** the conflict (median monthly CPI
  persistently >2%); depending on assumptions year-end CPI ultimately
  **surpasses 25%**, requiring a still-managed currency regime. USD/TRY 52
  eop-2026 kept.
- Key risk: residents' dollarization re-igniting pressure on the currency.

## Markets & positioning (pp. 20–23) — [not on dashboard: Bloomberg/CBRT securities stats]

- **Türkiye 5Y CDS ~300bp** (vs CDX EM ~188bp); the favorable carry gap has
  been erased despite orderly real appreciation.
- Offshore/onshore TRY rates repriced sharply (1M FWD ~44.7%, 1M OIS ~40.9%
  per chart labels).
- Sovereign sell-off: Eurobond spreads widened (USD TR 5Y ~7.6%); **TL 10Y
  yield moved to ~34%**. Hardest hit: **BIST banking equities in USD**.
- **CBRT response** (p. 22): suspended 1-week repo funding (policy rate held
  at 37%) pushing **effective cost of funding to 40%** (ON lending); sterilized
  excess TL via quotation + liquidity bills; **bought 214bn TL of TL sovereign
  bonds (3.7Y avg maturity) by Mar 26** out of 255bn planned for 2026 — to keep
  the bond market orderly and protect money-market-fund returns.
- **Foreigners' sell-off ~$35bn** (mid-week of Mar 25): $20–25bn carry +
  $10–15bn equity & bond. **Total carry stock $43bn at Mar 20** (≈$59bn end-Feb),
  of which foreigners ~$28bn (est. $15–20bn by mid-week). **Foreigners' share in
  local government bonds fell below 8%** (from 11% mid-Feb); share in equities
  ~36%.

## Residents & domestic sentiment (pp. 24–25) — partially on /liquidity (dollarization, residents' FC)

- Timely policy reaction prevented a domestic sentiment shock **so far**.
- Deposit rules keep TL savings attractive; MMF net outflows resumed but
  **smaller than the March-2025 domestic shock**.
- Residents' dollarization ratios broadly stable (deposit ~41%, incl.
  investment funds ~43% per chart); investment-fund volumes ~3.4 trn TL
  (TL funds ex-MMF), FC funds ~1.7 trn TL. **[fund data = TEFAS, not ingested]**

## Reserves (p. 26) — gross reserves on /liquidity; flow attribution [not on dashboard: BBVA estimate]

- Implicit FX sales >$50bn in March; falling gold prices also weigh on
  reserves; gold sales + swapped gold used to replenish high-quality liquid
  FX assets (HQLA ex-CB-swaps ~$70bn per chart).

## Financial conditions & credit (pp. 27–28) — credit growth on /weekly, rates on /rates; FCI [not on dashboard: proprietary]

- **Garanti BBVA FCI tightened again** post-conflict (CDS, yield slope, real
  FX among drivers).
- Deposit-rule-driven TL deposit rates (~47–52% region) push loan pricing up:
  consumer ~60%, commercial (ex cards/OD) mid-50s (chart labels).
- Credit growth stabilized at **34–35% 13w-annualized (FX-adj)**; recent
  overdraft/credit-card limits visibly decelerated GPL growth.

## Activity & nowcast (pp. 29–31) — GDP/labor on /economy; nowcast [not on dashboard: proprietary]

- 2025 GDP +3.6%, domestic-demand-driven; services the sector engine; the
  **persistent demand–supply gap** is why disinflation is slow.
- **Monthly GDP nowcast: Jan 3.0%, Feb 3.1%, Mar 3.4%** (info shares 100/40/18%)
  → 1Q26 ~3%.
- Labor: unemployment historically low (8.1–8.2% SA) but labor force declines
  and employment quality worsens; potential-LF-adjusted underutilization ~20%,
  broad measures up to ~30% (chart).

## Inflation & rates (pp. 32–33, 39) — CPI/expectations/real rate on /economy; OIS pricing [not on dashboard: Bloomberg]

- CPI trend worsened on food + early-year price adjustments **before** the
  conflict; **March CPI nowcast 2.2% m/m**; underlying trend ~2.4–2.6% SA m/m.
- Treasury introduced a **sliding-scale fuel-tax mechanism** to mute energy
  pass-through (absorbs up to ~$110/bbl Brent; costs up to 0.6% of GDP).
- Survey expectations (CBRT, March): market participants' **current year-end
  ~25.4%**, next year-end ~18.7%, 12m ~22–23%; households far higher (chart
  ~50% max-min band). Implied **ex-ante real policy rate expectation ~6%**.
- **12M OIS prices CBRT funding peaking ~40–41%** (27 Mar curve) — markets
  turned more hawkish through March, incl. pricing a hike in April.

## Fiscal (p. 34) — balances on /economy; impulse/rollover [not on dashboard]

- Primary **cash** balance posted a stronger surplus in Feb — room for fiscal
  maneuver against the conflict shock.
- BBVA estimates **~1.5pp of GDP negative fiscal impulse in 2025**; domestic
  debt rollover ratio ~80% in Jan–Feb (chart shows ~106–110% trailing).

## External balance (pp. 35, 41) — CA cuts + NEO on /economy

- External balance worsening predates the conflict; rising energy prices add
  concern. **Rule of thumb: every 10% rise in energy prices ⇒ ~0.3–0.4% of GDP
  CAB deterioration (~$5–7bn) within a year.**
- Methodology revision in the CA series + higher energy prices weigh on
  short-term CA forecasts (2026: −2.4% of GDP).

## Scenario sensitivities (pp. 37–38) — [not on dashboard: BBVA BVAR models]

- 1-sd **global geopolitical risk shock** (BVAR, Jan10–Dec19): TRY depreciates,
  BIST and IP fall, with effects compounding ~6 months out.
- **10% oil-supply-driven price hike**: cumulative IP −0.9–1.7pp (≈0.2–0.3pp
  GDP); CPI +1–1.5pp in one year (≈half absorbed by the fuel sliding scale).
- Energy baseline: **Brent avg $69 in 2026, $64 in 2027** (gas $12/$10 MMBtu);
  risk scenarios +30% and +60% vs base.

## Global backdrop (pp. 4–18, condensed)

Global growth resilient (3.2% 2025/2026, 3.3% 2027) on AI spending, fiscal
support and earlier rate cuts; Iran conflict lifted Brent and reignited
uncertainty. Markets price a **Fed hold-or-hike** this year and **≥3 ECB
hikes**; US tariffs fell somewhat after the IEEPA overruling (Section 122
replacements pending). Risks tilted down: prolonged energy disruption,
US institutional erosion, debt/credit markets, protectionism, China slowdown.
