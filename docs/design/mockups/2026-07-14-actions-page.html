Bank Actions

Record Jul 13, 2026 · KAP filings, classified daily · routine notices suppressed
compiled, not written

Turkish banks have reopened the offshore funding tap. Six of the eleven listed banks filed 67 debt-issuance approvals in the last ten weeks — Garanti and Vakıfbank repeatedly to foreign markets, İşbank for Tier-2 capital, TSKB back abroad for the first time in the window.

One bank is moving the other way: Şekerbank is raising equity, not debt — a rights issue approved by the board on 7 May and filed with the CMB on 3 June. Four banks were re-rated. Nothing else here is a decision; the rest of the KAP feed is coupon-payment plumbing, and this page counts it rather than showing it.

Suppressed as routine

coupon-payment notices, redemptions, issuance-ceiling maintenance and company-information forms — filings a bank makes because a rule says it must, not because anything happened. They are 27% of the KAP feed and they were the top of /disclosures every day.

The gap

what this page cannot yet say
amounts, maturities and coupons are not in this data

A KAP filing carries structured fields — nominal amount, ISIN, maturity, coupon — on its detail page. We hold only the title and the summary line, so this page can say that Garanti went to foreign markets four times but not for how much, for how long, or at what price. Until the detail form is scraped (src/news/kap.py), this page counts acts; it does not measure them.

The feed also only reaches back to 5 May 2026 — KAP's own window. A quarter-on-quarter issuance series needs a backfill before it can exist.
