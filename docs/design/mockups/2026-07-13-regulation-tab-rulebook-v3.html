Six numbers is not a regime. Most rules are announced in prose, with no machine-readable parameters — the credit growth caps among them. That is what the snapshot below is for, and why it is written by a model rather than a parser.

The regulatory snapshot Synthesized

moonshot-v1-128k · 88 instruments · 330-day window · generated 12 Jul 2026 · prompt 2026-05-29.v13-percat

The rules currently in force, in the sections of a BBVA-style sector report. This is the only part of the site where a language model writes — and it is here because it is the only tool that can read them: TCMB and BDDK publish most rules as prose, so a parser sees nothing. Every bullet carries the instrument it came from — click the code to read the regulator's own words. The model may write a sentence; it may never set a figure in the band above.

Why there is no “growth vs the cap” chart

The obvious chart — actual loan growth against the 8-week caps above — would be wrong. The caps apply to a restricted base: export, investment, agriculture, tradesmen, KOSGEB and CGF loans are exempt, and the limits are enforced bank-by-bank. Our weekly series is the whole book. Drawn naively, the sector appears to breach every cap in 42 of the last 49 weeks — which is an artefact of the base, not a finding. So the caps are stated, not charted, until the exempt base can be isolated.

Design notes — v3

Two corrections to v2. The page had drifted into the Liquidity tab — ₺4.06trn of reserves, the transmission wedge, the KKM unwind are balance-sheet analysis, and they belong next door. And the snapshot should never have been dissolved: I argued it duplicated the band, but the band only carries the six parameters a parser can read, and we had already proved that 9 rule changes in force have no machine-readable numbers at all.

The LLM is not a shortcut here — it is the only tool that works

TCMB publishes the credit growth caps as prose: 3% / 8 weeks for general-purpose and vehicle loans, 4.5% commercial, 0.5% FX. Our parser sees 342 characters and no table. The model reads them. That is not a convenience — without it those rules are simply invisible.

Three tiers, and the page says which is which

Compiled — parsed and reconciled; no model touches a figure. Synthesized — the model writes, and every bullet links to the instrument, so any claim is one click from the regulator's own words. Absent — 2 of 7 sections have no source; they stay empty.

The empty sections are the trust

Asked to write the CAR and credit-card sections anyway, the model leaked adjacent rules and fabricated tier tables. So the summarizer skips them. An empty section a reader can see beats a plausible one they cannot check — and it is the strongest evidence that the sections which are filled were sourced.

What I checked and did not draw

With the caps now readable, the tempting chart is growth-vs-cap. It would be wrong: the caps exempt export, investment, agriculture, KOSGEB and CGF lending and bind bank-by-bank, while our series is the whole book.

Drawn naively the sector "breaches" every cap in 42 of 49 weeks — an artefact of the base, not a finding. Stated, not charted.

Kept from v2: two visuals, demoted

The corridor's path (a rate is not a level) and what the reserve rule actually holds (the lira ratio built from 0.02% in 2022). Both illustrate an instrument, so both stay — as marks beside the rules, not as the page's spine.
