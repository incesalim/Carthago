The composition chart

Deposits by ownership · W/E 03 Jul 2026 · 166 weeks drawn from D1 — every figure computed

A stacked area is the site's default answer to "who holds the book". It is the wrong answer to almost every question the titles ask. The chart below carries the finding "the book shrank ₺0.40 trn in the week" — a move worth 1.3% of one band, about three pixels. Meanwhile the shape it does draw so confidently — a book that nearly tripled — is mostly the lira: in constant prices the same book is 9.2% smaller than it was in May 2023.

The readout rail replaces the tooltip. It is always populated — the latest week at rest, the hovered week on hover — so no value is gated behind a mouse, and nothing is ever drawn on top of the mark. Hover a row to isolate its band.

Mix is the default because it is the only view that is inflation-neutral: a band can only grow by taking share. Levels and Real are one click away — carried over, never deleted.

What changed this week?

Δ deposits by group · ₺ trn · the title, drawn

This strip is the chart the headline needed. The sector lost ₺0.40 trn; the state banks lost ₺0.46 trn — so the rest of the sector gained ₺0.06 trn. The stack could not show a sign, let alone an offset.

Δ4W and Δ13W sit beside it because one week is noise: over 13 weeks the sector added ₺1.65 trn, and State added ₺0.62 trn. A single-week finding that reverses at 13 weeks is a finding that has to say so.

Is the book actually growing?

total deposits indexed to May 2023 = 100 · nominal vs CPI-deflated
NOMINAL ×2.86REAL ×0.91 CPI 52W +28.2%DEPOSITS 52W +32.4% → +3.3% real

The wedge is the whole objection. The nominal line — the one the stack draws — ends at 286. Deflated by CPI, the same book ends at 91: nine per cent below where it started. Three years of "growth" is the money losing value, not the sector winning deposits.

The last 52 weeks are the exception that proves the mark: +32.4% nominal against +28.2% CPI is a real +3.3% — genuinely positive, and invisible on a nominal stack that rises either way.

03

The rules this sets for every StackedArea

the chart class, not this one chart
  1. R1
    Composition is a share chart; the stack is not a trend chart.

    Default the mark to mix (bands to 100%). Levels stay reachable — the size of the book is a real question — but they stop being the thing the reader meets first.

  2. R2
    Any nominal ₺ level ships with its real twin.

    In a 28% CPI regime a nominal level chart is a chart of the deflator. real is a view, not a footnote — and the deflator's lag is printed, never hidden.

  3. R3
    The finding gets a mark of its own.

    If the title says "−₺0.40 trn in the week", a Δ strip is drawn under the composition. A finding smaller than the axis resolution is not a finding the chart has made.

  4. R4
    Colour follows the entity, never the code table.

    One group, one colour, across weekly and monthly charts alike. seriesColor() must key on the resolved group, not on a numeric code whose meaning changes between bulletins.

  5. R5
    The readout rail replaces the floating tooltip.

    Values live in a fixed column beside the mark — populated at rest, updated on hover, never occluding. The crosshair carries the date back to the axis. Identity is direct-labelled at the band end, so no legend chip is the only key to a colour.

  6. R6
    Bands are separated by the sheet, not by a border.

    A 2px gap in the sheet colour between fills; hairline grid; no band outlines. Hovering a row or a band drops the others to 28% — emphasis by contrast, not by chrome.
