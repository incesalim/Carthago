The finding

The page you are replacing says its latest decision landed on 9 July. That decision was the replacement of an SSL certificate on the CBRT website. Of the 7 “instruments” in its headline count, 5 are not regulation at all. The two that are had their parameters sitting in the database — before value, after value, and the date they bind — unread.

  • It counts, it does not read. The vitals band reports feed volume, recency and a keyword topic. Not one of the four is a fact about the regime.
  • The rules are already here. 258 of 264 TCMB releases carry full body text, averaging 2,639 characters — with before/after parameter tables and the date they bind.
  • So is the answer. The weekly Kimi briefing already extracts the corridor and the ratios, with source ids — then prints them as bullets in a box, below the fold.
  • And the clock is wrong. BDDK board decisions are sorted by the date we scraped them, not the date they were taken — a mean error of 309 days.

Design notes — what changed, and why

The Desk skeleton is unchanged: a computed brief above carried-over evidence. What changes is what the signature element is about. The band stops describing the feed and starts stating the regime — which the database already knew.

The vitals became a rulebook

Six cells, but they are parameters a bank complies with, not counts of announcements: the corridor (37 / 40 / 35.5) and the reserve ratios (32 / 28, and the 2.5% add-on now dead). Each cell shows the value it replaced, the date it binds, and the instrument that set it.

The chart is the page's own text, drawn

48 MPC decisions parsed out of body_text — the full cycle from the 8.5% trough to the 50% peak. No new source, no new fetch. The page has stored this for four years and never drawn a line.

The clock is corrected, not hidden

Was: everything sorted by scrape date, so a June-2024 decision reads as March-2026 news.

Now: keyed on the decision date printed in the title, with the publication lag drawn — and the March 2026 backlog dump visible as the comb it is.

The gaps are printed

33 of 2,197 decision numbers; BDDK bodies on 162 of 603 items; no effective-date column. A page that states what it cannot see is worth more than one that prints a tidy count of a magazine and an SSL certificate.

Superseding the first mockup

The earlier regulations mockup proposed an implementation calendar, impact ratings and an action horizon assigning work to “Treasury” and “Compliance” — all of it invented, and addressed to a compliance officer who is not our reader.

Its one good idea, the effective date, is real and recoverable — it is in the prose. So it is shown, and flagged as the extraction work this design implies.

Carry-over contract honoured

The Kimi briefing and the raw TCMB/BDDK feeds are not deleted. They move under In depth, lose their boxes, and become evidence for the band that now cites them.
