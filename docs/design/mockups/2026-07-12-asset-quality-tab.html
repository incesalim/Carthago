The NPL ratio prints 2.69% and looks calm. The bad-loan stock grew 79.8% — 34.9% in real terms.

The ratio barely moved (+0.70pp) because its denominator is inflating. Bad loans alone would have pushed it to 3.97%; a loan book growing 36.6% took −1.06pp back off. And that loan growth is not real credit — in real, constant-FX terms the book shrank 2.1%.
