What changed on Capital — and one number that was wrong

The vitals band stays. But this page had a story it wasn't telling and a number it was getting wrong, and both fall out of its own data. Every figure below is computed from the deployed page's series plus the audited §4 filings behind them.

The page calls a cliff an “easing”

Capital adequacy went 19.69% → 16.77% between December and January — a −2.92pp step in one month, the largest move in all 76 months of record (the next biggest is 2.44pp), and every ownership group fell together, by 2.7 to 5.4 points. The live page describes this as “capital adequacy eased to 16.3% (−1.2pp over 12m)”. A one-month structural break averaged into a twelve-month trend. Was: a finding-title that smooths the discontinuity away.

…and then extrapolates the average

The “Headroom” section takes that 12-month drift and projects a floor date. Extrapolating a step is meaningless: split the year and it is the step (−2.92pp) plus everything else (+1.75pp) — ex-step, the sector built capital through 2025. The redesign prints the split, then extrapolates only the post-step slope (Jan→May, −1.28pp/yr) and names the window it used. Was: four Stat boxes projecting a floor from a broken premise.

The CET1 the site prints is wrong

sectorCapitalRatios sums each component across banks but divides by the RWA of all banks — so a bank with a NULL component still contributes its RWA to the denominator. ISCTR's CET1 is missing for 2025Q4 and 2026Q1 while its RWA (10.6% of the sector's) is present, dragging the published CET1 to 10.56% when the true figure is 11.79%. It is recoverable from the row itself: Tier-1 − AT1 = ₺398.6bn, which reproduces ISCTR's own printed CET1 ratio (11.74%) exactly. This is a code fix, not a design one — and it is live right now.

The buffer is smaller than the hybrid stack

CAR 16.02% = CET1 11.79 + AT1 1.68 + Tier-2 2.55. The buffer over the 12% minimum is 4.02pp; the AT1 and Tier-2 stack is 4.23pp. Strip the instruments and the sector's ratio is 11.79% — below the minimum it has to meet. AT1 and Tier-2 legitimately count toward the 12%, and CET1 has its own lower floor, so this is not a breach — it is a statement about what the cushion is made of: 17 of 34 banks hold common equity below 12%.

A stack is the right mark here

Unlike the reserve buffer on /liquidity — where a component goes negative and a stacked area would have lied — CET1, AT1 and Tier-2 are all positive and sum to total capital by construction. So the composition chart is a stacked area, and the per-bank chart nests the hybrid stack on top of common equity. Same question, different data, different mark.
