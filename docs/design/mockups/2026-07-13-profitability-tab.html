    What changed on Profitability — and why it is deploy-ready

    The vitals band stays. The page already says returns are negative in real terms; what it never says is where the return comes from. Both new findings are computed from tables we already ingest (`income_statement`, `balance_sheet`), refresh with the monthly bulletin, and are pinned by a reconciliation that fails loudly if the source layout changes.

    The margin isn’t earned. It’s collected.

    36.9% of the deposit base is demand money that pays nothing. The sector pays 33.1% on the deposits it does pay for, so its blended cost is only 20.9% — 12.2pp of free funding. Priced at the rate it pays everyone else, the demand book is worth ₺3.19trn a year against a total sector profit of ₺1.01trn. The free money is worth 3.1× the entire profit of the banking system, and has run 2.6×–4.5× for eighteen months.

    It is a sizing device, not a forecast — and the page says so

    Demand deposits are not literally free: servicing them (branches, payments, cards) is part of the 54.8% cost/income the page now prints. And if banks paid market rates on them, the balance sheet would not stay the same. The claim is only what the arithmetic supports: what the free funding is worth at the sector’s own paid rate. That is the difference between an insight and a headline.

    The income statement is cumulative — so de-cumulate it

    Every ratio the page prints (ROE, ROA, NIM, OPEX) is a year-to-date figure annualized. The new bridge subtracts last month’s YTD from this month’s to get the month alone: NII +₺98bn y/y, yet profit fell — trading (−₺42bn) and costs (−₺55bn) took it. That is invisible in a YTD average. Was: six Stat boxes restating the vitals.

    The reconciliation is the deploy gate

    The bridge is built from fixed item_order positions in the BDDK statement. If BDDK renumbers a line, the sum silently drifts — so the page reconciles the bridge against the reported net-profit line every render (today: ₺0.0000trn difference) and prints a data-quality flag instead of the chart if it breaks. A page that must survive a cron has to fail loudly, not quietly.

    Two theses I tested and threw away

    (1) “The run-rate is below the YTD print.” It is not — de-cumulated monthly ROE swings 21–44% with no trend. (2) “A depositor earns more than a shareholder.” Also false: 20.9% vs 25.8%. Building on real series is what killed both before they shipped.
